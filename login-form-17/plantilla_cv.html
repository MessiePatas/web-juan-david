HOW MINERS INFLATE GRAPHICS CARD PRICES

HOW MINERS INFLATE GRAPHICS CARD PRICES:

Over time, having a video card has gone from being something normal to being a privilege. This is because there is currently a shortage of graphics cards in the market, which means that there is no supply, so the prices of these graphics cards are inflated to gigantic prices. But why are miners interested? To know this, it is necessary to know why miners use video cards, since they use them to mine cryptocurrencies specifically ethereum. That said, miners use graphs to mine cryptocurrencies, so they need a large number of cards to do this process and end up running out of stock of the cards, making a card that normally costs $475 more than $800. . That is to say that in theory the price of the graphs depends on the price of the cryptocurrencies if the cryptocurrencies go down it will make the miners less interested in buying graphs and therefore there will be stock and it will allow the price to go down, but it is important to keep in mind account that it is very difficult for the price to normalize. But what measures have companies taken in the face of this shortage of graphics cards? The companies have chosen to cap the graphics cards before mining but this only applies to new models, that is, the models that have a cap will not have price inflation but the graphics cards that do not have a cap will continue with the inflated price, in addition to the fact that the layered cards have a lower performance than normal causing that if you want to have a powerful graphics you have to pay large sums of money but if you want to save money you have to search for low performance
